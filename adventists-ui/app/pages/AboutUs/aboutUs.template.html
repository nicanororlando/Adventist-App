Seventh-day Adventists, with more than 21.9 million members worldwide, is a Protestant Christian church organized in 1863 in the United States. This originates from the movement led by Guillermo Miller, who emphasized the need to preach about the soon return of Jesus Christ to this world. The South American headquarters of the Seventh-day Adventist Church is responsible for administrative coordination in eight countries with more than two million members. The Seventh-day Adventist Church has the following Mission and Vision:

Mission
Make disciples of all nations, communicating the everlasting gospel in the context of the three angels' messages based on Revelation 14:6-12, inviting them to accept Jesus as their personal Savior and to join his remnant church, instructing people to serve him Him as Lord and to prepare them for his soon return.
Vision
In harmony with the great prophecies of the Scriptures, we understand that the climax of God's plan is to restore all of his creation to complete harmony with his perfect will and justice.

Adventist Lifestyle

Adventists believe in a life of consecration to God in the physical, psychological, emotional and spiritual aspects. His lifestyle is based on eight natural remedies: water, rest, physical exercise, sunlight, fresh air, balanced nutrition, temperance, and hope in God. One of the principles that distinguishes them is rest on Saturday, a day set aside by God for a greater and better relationship with their children. Adventists trust God to work in all areas of their lives and are confident that Christ will return soon to reward those who have trusted in Him.

Communication with God

Adventists believe that the best way to communicate with God is through prayer, Bible study, and good testimony. Prayer consists of seeking God's presence every morning, daily Bible study, is recognized as a way of listening to divine guidance, and testimony is the natural result of this communion as a habit in the life of each believer.
